Why ElevenLabs over Bhashini

Bhashini's 24-hour registration approval is structurally incompatible with a 24-hour hackathon — you could burn 12 hours waiting. AI4Bharat requires a ~300MB model download and local inference setup. ElevenLabs works immediately with an API key, has a free 10,000 char/month tier, and Multilingual v2 produces natural-sounding Telugu output. The only tradeoff: ElevenLabs is cloud-only, so test latency at Hour 0.

Why 3 parallel agents instead of single advisory call

The single advisory.py call made voice feel architecturally separate from the dashboard. With 3 parallel agents, the voice path mirrors the dashboard debate — the same Season-Optimist, Season-Pessimist, and Risk Analyst that power the WebSocket stream also power the voice response. This makes the architecture story stronger in the pitch: "the same AI debate that drives the dashboard also answers your voice query."

Q&A Preparation · Anticipate the Judges

The questions they will ask.

Your data is seasonal, not daily. How is this actionable for a farmer who needs to decide this week?
The seasonal view answers the most important question first: is the current price above or below the MSP floor? That single signal — combined with 4 years of seasonal context — tells you whether this is a historically good or bad time to sell. We deliberately built around the data we actually have rather than pretending to have daily mandi data we don't. The right insight from real data beats a fake forecast from missing data.
Why do you need 3 AI agents for this? Couldn't one LLM call do it?
One LLM call would give you one perspective. The Season-Optimist, Pessimist, and Risk Analyst are adversarially designed — they look at the same data through different lenses. The Optimist looks for price uptrend signals. The Pessimist looks for MSP-proximity risk. The Risk Analyst weights Kharif vs Rabi exposure. The synthesis is only trustworthy because all three disagreements are surfaced as a conflict score. You can't get that from a single prompt.
How accurate is the recommendation?
The recommendation is confidence-labeled, not claimed-accurate. We surface a High/Moderate/Low confidence label and a conflict score when agents disagree. We chose not to quote a single accuracy number because the data is seasonal — there's no rolling-origin backtest equivalent. Honest uncertainty framing is more useful to the farmer than false precision.
Why not just use ChatGPT?
Three reasons: ChatGPT doesn't have our 4-season MSP-vs-price dataset, doesn't run a structured multi-agent debate with adversarial perspectives, and doesn't deliver the output in Telugu voice. Also: our entire stack runs on free tiers. Zero rupees.
What happens if WiFi dies?
We built a canned-data mode. [Switch to ?demo=canned]. Pre-computed full responses for 3 commodities, zero external API calls. The demo keeps running. Let me show you now.
Can this scale to more commodities?
Yes. The pipeline is commodity-agnostic — add a new season report CSV, run the normalizer, and the system picks it up automatically. The agent prompts are parameterized by commodity name and season data. We deliberately scoped to 14 commodities for the demo, not because of architecture limits.
Meta-Strategy for Q&A

Every answer ends with something concrete: a number, a specific technical choice, or "let me show you." Never end on an abstract claim. If a judge asks something you didn't build: "We didn't build that in this MVP, but the architecture supports it via [specific extension point]." Never bluff. Never hedge without an alternative.
